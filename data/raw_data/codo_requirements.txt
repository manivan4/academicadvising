Knowledge Base: Purdue CS CODO Requirements (2025-2026)

===========================================================================
1. Executive Summary & Policy Overview
===========================================================================
Space-Restricted Status: Computer Science is a space-restricted program.
Meeting the minimum requirements does NOT guarantee admission.

Admissions Basis: Decisions are made on a Space Available Basis Only,
following a holistic academic review.

Decision Finality: All CODO decisions are final and cannot be appealed.

Catalog Term Logic: Students are bound by the requirements of their
"Catalog Term" (the semester they started at Purdue). This document
covers current standards (2024-2026).

===========================================================================
2. General Eligibility Requirements ("Gatekeeper" Criteria)
===========================================================================
ALL of the following must be true before course grades are even considered.
Failure to meet any one of these makes a student immediately ineligible.

  - Minimum Semesters: Must have completed at least 1 semester at Purdue
    West Lafayette campus.
  - Minimum Credits: Must have completed at least 12 graded credit hours
    at Purdue West Lafayette campus.
  - Minimum Cumulative GPA: 2.75 or higher.
  - Academic Standing: Student must NOT be on academic probation.

===========================================================================
3. Required Courses (ALL must be completed with the required grade)
===========================================================================

--- A. Computer Science (MANDATORY — NO SUBSTITUTES) ---

CS 18000 — Problem Solving and Object-Oriented Programming
  Required grade: B or better.
  THIS COURSE IS MANDATORY. A student who has not taken CS 18000, or who
  has taken it but earned below a B, does NOT meet this requirement.
  There is no substitute course for CS 18000.
  Note: Non-CS majors can typically only enroll in CS 18000 during Spring
  or Summer semesters.
  "Highest Consideration" threshold: A- or higher.

--- B. Mathematics (ONE APPROVED COURSE REQUIRED) ---

A student needs a B or better in ANY ONE of the following approved courses.
They do NOT need one from each subject — just one course from this list:

  MA 16100  (Anlytc Geomtry & Calc I)
  MA 16500  (Calculus I)
  MA 16200  (Anlytc Geomtry & Calc II)
  MA 16600  (Calculus II)
  MA 26100  (Multivariate Calculus)
  MA 27101  (Honors Calculus III)
  MA 26500  (Linear Algebra)
  MA 35100  (Elementary Linear Algebra)

  NOT APPROVED: MA 15800, MA 16010, MA 16020 do NOT satisfy this requirement.

  Example A: Student has B in MA 16500 → Mathematics MET.
  Example B: Student has B in MA 26500 only → Mathematics MET.
  Example C: Student has only MA 15800 → Mathematics NOT MET (invalid course).


===========================================================================
4. Competitive Thresholds ("Highest Consideration")
===========================================================================
Due to high competition, students who only meet the minimums are frequently
denied. To be in the most competitive tier, a student should have ALL of:

  - CS 18000 grade: A- or higher.
  - All math course grades: B or higher.
  - Cumulative GPA: 3.0 or higher.

===========================================================================
5. Strict Academic Rules & Limitations
===========================================================================
Rule of Two: Only the FIRST or SECOND attempt of any required CS or Math
course will be considered for CODO. A "W" (Withdrawal) counts as an
attempt. If a student fails to earn a B within two attempts at a required
course and has no passing alternative, they are PERMANENTLY INELIGIBLE
to CODO into CS.

Purdue Campus Requirement: All CS and Math courses must be taken for a
letter grade on a Purdue campus. Transfer courses are not accepted.

No Supplemental Data: The review committee does not consider personal
projects, resumes, or letters of recommendation. The decision is purely
academic.

===========================================================================
6. Deadlines & Timeline
===========================================================================
Application Deadline: 5:00 PM on the Thursday of Finals Week for the
Fall, Spring, and Summer terms.

Submission Process: Students must contact their current academic advisor
to initiate the CODO request via the "Curricular Change Request" (CCR)
system.

Review Period: Applications are evaluated the week following finals after
grades are posted.

Notification: Students are notified of the decision via their official
Purdue email.

===========================================================================
7. Strategic Guidance for Ineligible Students
===========================================================================
If a student does not meet the CODO requirements, suggest these paths:

  - CS Minor: Requires a C or better in CS 18000. Allows access to some
    upper-level CS courses in "off-peak" semesters.
  - Data Science / Artificial Intelligence: Similar requirements but
    slightly different space availability profiles.
  - Applied Statistics: For students interested in the mathematical side
    of computing.

===========================================================================
8. Evaluation Logic (Internal Checklist for the AI)
===========================================================================
When a transcript is provided, check each item in order. Mark INELIGIBLE
the moment any item fails — do NOT override this with a passing status.

  Step 1 — GPA: Is cumulative GPA >= 2.75? If not → INELIGIBLE.
  Step 2 — Semesters/Credits: Has the student completed 1 semester and
            12+ graded credits at Purdue WL? If not → INELIGIBLE.
  Step 3 — Academic Standing: Is the student NOT on probation? If unsure,
            note as unknown.
  Step 4 — CS 18000: Does the transcript show CS 18000 with a B or better?
            If CS 18000 is absent OR grade is below B → INELIGIBLE.
  Step 5 — Mathematics: Does the transcript show a B or better in ANY ONE of:
            MA 16100, MA 16500, MA 16200, MA 16600, MA 26100, MA 27101,
            MA 26500, MA 35100? Only ONE of these courses is needed.
            If no approved course has a B or better → INELIGIBLE.

  FINAL STATUS: ELIGIBLE only if ALL 5 steps pass. NOT ELIGIBLE otherwise.

  Tier Classification (if ELIGIBLE):
    Tier 1 — Highest Consideration: A- in CS 18000 AND GPA >= 3.0.
    Tier 2 — Meets Minimums: B in CS 18000 AND GPA >= 2.75 (but < 3.0).
