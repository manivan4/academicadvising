Knowledge Base: Purdue CS CODO Requirements (2025-2026)
1. Executive Summary & Policy Overview
Space-Restricted Status: Computer Science is a space-restricted program. Meeting the minimum requirements does not guarantee admission.

Admissions Basis: Decisions are made on a Space Available Basis Only following a holistic academic review.

Decision Finality: All CODO decisions are final and cannot be appealed.

Catalog Term Logic: Students are bound by the requirements of their "Catalog Term" (the semester they started at Purdue). This document focuses on current standards (2024-2026).

2. General Eligibility Requirements
Before specific course grades are considered, a student must meet these baseline "gatekeeper" criteria:

Minimum Semesters: 1 semester completed at the Purdue West Lafayette campus.

Minimum Credits: 12 graded credit hours completed at the Purdue West Lafayette campus.

Minimum Cumulative GPA: 2.75 or higher.

Academic Standing: Students must not be on academic probation.

3. Core Course Requirements
Students must complete the following courses with a B or better to be eligible for consideration:

A. Computer Science Component
CS 18000 (Problem Solving and Object-Oriented Programming):

Grade Required: B or better.

Note: Students wishing for "Highest Consideration" should aim for an A- or higher.

Access: Non-majors can typically only take CS 18000 during the Spring or Summer semesters.

B. Mathematics Component
Approved Calculus Course: B or better in one of the following:

MA 16100 or MA 16500 (Calculus I)

MA 16200 or MA 16600 (Calculus II)

MA 26100 or MA 27101 (Multivariate Calculus)

MA 26500 or MA 35100 (Linear Algebra)

Note: MA 15800, MA 16010, and MA 16020 do not fulfill the CODO calculus requirement for Computer Science.

4. Competitive Thresholds (Highest Consideration)
Due to extreme competition, students meeting only the minimums are frequently denied. To be in the most competitive tier, students should meet the following:

CS 18000 Grade: A- or higher.

Calculus Grade: B or higher.

Cumulative GPA: 3.0 or higher.

5. Strict Academic Rules & Limitations
The "Rule of Two": Only the first or second attempt of any required CS or Math course will be considered for CODO.

A "W" (Withdrawal) counts as an attempt.

If a student fails to get a B in two tries, they are permanently ineligible to CODO into CS.

Purdue Campus Requirement: All CS and Math courses used for CODO must be taken for a letter grade on a Purdue campus.

No Supplemental Data: The review committee does not consider personal projects, resumes, or letters of recommendation. The decision is purely academic.

6. Deadlines & Timeline
Application Deadline: 5:00 PM on the Thursday of Finals Week for the Fall, Spring, and Summer terms.

Submission Process: Students must contact their current academic advisor to initiate the CODO request via the "Curricular Change Request" (CCR) system.

Review Period: Applications are evaluated the week following finals after grades are posted.

Notification: Students are notified of the decision via their official Purdue email.

7. Strategic Guidance for Ineligible Students
If a student does not meet the requirements, the RAG bot should suggest these alternatives:

CS Minor: Requires a C or better in CS 18000. Allows access to some upper-level CS courses in "off-peak" semesters.

Data Science / Artificial Intelligence: Similar requirements but slightly different space availability profiles.

Applied Statistics: For students interested in the mathematical side of computing.

Knowledge Base Logic for RAG (Internal Note)
When assessing a query, the model should follow this retrieval logic:

Identify GPA: If < 2.75, flag as ineligible.

Check CS 180: If grade < B, or > 2 attempts, flag as ineligible.

Check Calc: Verify the course is on the approved list (MA 161/165/162/166/261/351). If grade < B, flag as ineligible.

Categorize: * Tier 1: Meets "Highest Consideration" (A- in 180, 3.0+ GPA).

Tier 2: Meets Minimums (B in 180, 2.75+ GPA).

Tier 3: Ineligible.
