Purdue University: Mechanical Engineering CODO Requirements (2025-2026)

1. General Eligibility Criteria To be considered for a Change of Degree
   Objective (CODO) into the School of Mechanical Engineering (ME), students
   must meet the following baseline institutional requirements:

Minimum Semesters: 1 semester completed at Purdue University.

Minimum Credits: 15 credit hours earned at the Purdue West Lafayette or
Indianapolis campus.

Academic Standing: Students must be in "Good Academic Standing" (not on academic
notice/probation).

Cumulative GPA: A minimum cumulative GPA of 3.4 is required for eligibility.

2. Course-Specific GPA & Grade Requirements The CODO process for ME involves two
   distinct "baskets" of course requirements, each with its own GPA threshold.

Basket A: Professional Engineering Coursework (3.4 GPA Target) Students must
have a 3.4 GPA or higher across a minimum of 12 credit hours in the following
courses:

Mathematics: * MA 26100 (Multivariate Calculus) OR

MA 26200 (Linear Algebra and Differential Equations) OR

MA 26500 (Linear Algebra) AND MA 26600 (Ordinary Differential Equations)

Physics: * PHYS 24100 (Electricity and Optics) OR * PHYS 27200 (Electric and
Magnetic Interactions)

Engineering Core: * At least one 200-level engineering discipline course (e.g.,
ME 20000, ME 27000, ECE 20100, CE 29800, AAE 20300).

Note: All 200-level engineering courses taken will be included in this 3.4 GPA
calculation.

Basket B: Foundational Coursework (2.0 GPA Target / C- Minimum) Students must
earn a C- or better and maintain a minimum 2.0 GPA in the following:

Chemistry: CHM 11500 (General Chemistry)

Physics: PHYS 17200 (Modern Mechanics)

Calculus I: MA 16100 or MA 16500

Calculus II: MA 16200 or MA 16600

Oral Communication: COM 11400 (or equivalent Foundational Outcome course)

Written Communication: ENGL 10600 (or equivalent Foundational Outcome course)

Science Selective: Typically CS 15900 (C Programming) or CHM 11600.

Engineering Transitions: * ENGR 13100 & ENGR 13200 (Transforming Ideas to
Innovation I & II) OR

Honors equivalents (ENGR 141/142 or ENGR 161/162) OR

EPICS/VIP pathways (ENGR 13300 + EPCS/VIP sequences).

3. Competitive Selection & Holistic Review Meeting the minimum requirements does
   not guarantee admission.

Space Availability: Admission is highly competitive and offered on a Space
Available Basis Only.

STEM GPA: A "STEM GPA" (average of all engineering, science, and math courses)
of 3.4 or higher is strictly monitored and heavily weighted in the holistic
review.

Credit Load: While 12 credits of professional coursework are required, the
admissions committee prefers students who have completed at least 15 credits in
their most recent semester.

4. The CODO Process & Timeline Advisor Consultation: Students must meet with
   their current academic advisor to express interest.

ME CODO Meeting: Students are strongly encouraged to email
MEundergrad@purdue.edu to meet with an ME-specific advisor to verify progress.

Submission: The online CODO request must be initiated by the current advisor no
later than Finals Week of the semester.

Review: Transcripts are evaluated the week after final grades post for the Fall,
Spring, and Summer terms.

Notification: Students are notified of the decision via Purdue email, typically
the week before the next semester begins.
